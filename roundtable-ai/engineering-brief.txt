PRODUCT: Apex ATE-75 Outdoor TV Enclosure - Engineering Design Review

REQUEST: Please review this enclosure design for a 75" outdoor TV. Identify flaws, risks, and improvements needed for thermal management, weather protection, structural integrity, and manufacturability.

---

DESIGN OVERVIEW:
Weatherproof aluminum enclosure for 75" commercial displays, using front-breathing passive/active cooling (no AC unit).

---

EXTERNAL DIMENSIONS:
- Width: 1760mm
- Height: 1040mm
- Depth: 120mm
- Weight target: ~25kg

CONSTRUCTION:
- Material: 3mm aluminum sheet (6061-T6 or 5052-H32)
- Finish: Powder-coated or anodized
- Joints: Welded corners, sealed
- Bezel frame: 35mm width all sides

---

THERMAL DESIGN:

Concept: Front-breathing convection with fan-assisted exhaust

Airflow Path:
1. Cool ambient air enters via bottom intake slot
2. Air passes through 25mm gap between glass and TV
3. Heat rises naturally (convection)
4. Hot air exits via top exhaust slot + fan assist

Components:
- Bottom intake slot: 1700mm × 15mm (25,500 mm² open area)
- Top exhaust slot: 1700mm × 20mm (34,000 mm² open area)
- Fans: 4× 60mm diameter, 15mm depth, thermostat-controlled
- Air gap: 25mm between glass and TV back

Heat Load Estimate:
- 75" commercial TV: ~200-350W power consumption
- Assume 70% converted to heat: 140-245W dissipation required
- Summer ambient: up to 45°C
- Target internal temp: <60°C (TV safe operating range)

Fan Specs (placeholder):
- 60mm × 15mm slim fans
- ~20-30 CFM each at full speed
- Thermostat trigger: 45°C internal
- PWM speed control based on temp

WEATHER PROTECTION:

Target Rating: IP55
- Protected from dust ingress
- Protected from water jets (rain, sprinklers)

Sealing Strategy:
- Glass panel: Rubber gasket compression seal
- Cable entries: 2× 30mm holes with rubber grommets
- Intake/exhaust slots: Louvered design to deflect direct water
- All joints: Silicone sealant + welded

Drainage:
- Small weep holes at bottom corners (1-2mm) for condensation escape

3. Is the gasket seal sufficient for the glass panel?
4. UV exposure - will powder coat withstand desert sun?
5. Salt spray resistance for coastal installations?
6. Condensation management - are weep holes enough?

STRUCTURAL DESIGN:

Frame Construction:
- 3mm aluminum walls (top, bottom, sides, back)
- 35mm bezel frame provides rigidity
- No internal frame/skeleton

Mounting:
- VESA 600×400mm pattern on rear panel
- 4× M8 threaded inserts
- Enclosure hangs on wall mount, TV mounts inside to VESA

Glass Panel:
- 6mm tempered safety glass
- Dimensions: 1686mm × 966mm
- Retained by bezel frame, sealed with gasket
- Removable for TV access

MANUFACTURABILITY:

Proposed Process:

- Bend/brake to shape
- TIG weld corners
- Powder coat finish
- Install gaskets, hardware, fans

Assembly:
- Glass panel removable (front access)
- Fans accessible from front when glass removed
- TV installs on internal VESA bracket

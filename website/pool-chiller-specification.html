Key Difference from APE-P (Pump Enclosures)
Heaters and chillers require significant airflow for operation. This enclosure provides weather protection and aesthetic screening rather than full acoustic sealing. Louvered panels allow required airflow while reducing noise by 50-60%.

The APE-HC (Apex Pool Equipment - Heater/Chiller) Series provides weather protection and noise reduction for pool heating and cooling equipment. Unlike the sealed APE-P pump enclosures, these use louvered panels to maintain essential equipment airflow.

1.2 Design Philosophy

Why Louvered?

Heat pumps and chillers require continuous airflow across condenser coils. Sealed enclosures would cause overheating and compressor failure. Angled louvers provide weather protection while maintaining airflow.

Why 50-60% Noise Reduction?

Full 80% reduction is impossible with open airflow paths. However, louvers provide directional screening - noise is redirected away from living areas. Combined with foam strips, this achieves meaningful reduction.

3 Airflow Requirements

üí®
Airflow is Critical
Heat pumps and chillers exchange heat with ambient air. Restricting airflow reduces efficiency by 10-30% and can cause compressor failure. The enclosure must allow free air circulation.

3.1 Heat Pump/Chiller Airflow Path

Air drawn in through sides (condenser coils) ‚Üì Refrigerant absorbs/rejects heat ‚Üì Fan exhausts air upward (hot in cooling mode, cold in heating mode) ‚Üì Hot/cold air must escape freely through top

3.4 Gas Heater Ventilation

üî•
Gas Heater Special Requirements
Gas heaters require combustion air intake and exhaust flue clearance. The enclosure must have adequate low-level ventilation for combustion air and clearance around the exhaust flue as per manufacturer specs.
  • Combustion air: Low-level intake required (typically 100 cm¬≤ per 10kW)
  • Flue clearance: Maintain manufacturer clearances (typically 300-600mm above flue)
  • CO detection: Consider adding CO detector for enclosed gas heaters

4.3 Rain and Weather Protection

Rain Protection

  • 45-degree louver angle sheds rain outward
  • Drip edges on each blade
  • No direct rain entry at angles up to 45¬∞
  • Base drain holes for any condensation

Sun Protection

  • Louvers shade equipment from direct sun
  • Reduces solar heat gain on equipment
  • Can extend equipment lifespan
  • RAL 7016 dark finish absorbs less heat

4.4 Directional Noise Screening

While the enclosure cannot fully contain noise (airflow paths remain open), louvered panels direct sound:

  • Front panel (toward living area): Dense louver pattern with maximum foam backing
  • Side panels: Standard louver pattern
  • Back panel (toward boundary): Can be solid if clearance allows (best noise reduction)
  • Strategic positioning: Orient solid/dense side toward noise-sensitive areas

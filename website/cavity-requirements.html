v5.3.0 Integrated Bezel Design - Simplified Recess Requirements

The v5.3.0 design exhausts air through front-facing recessed slots in the top bezel. Unlike rear-exhaust designs, hot air exits forward into the room - NOT into the cavity. This significantly simplifies recess installation.

2. Why v5.3.0 is Different (Front Exhaust)

Front-Facing Exhaust Slots

The v5.3.0 integrated bezel design has exhaust slots recessed 30-40mm behind the front bezel face. Hot air exits forward into the room, not into the cavity behind.

  • No recirculation risk: Exhaust air goes forward, intake air comes from below the enclosure front
  • Minimal top gap needed: 20mm provides fitting tolerance - not thermal requirement
  • Simpler installation: No critical clearance to enforce

Rear Depth (55mm minimum)

The enclosure is 160mm deep. With 55mm behind it (total 215mm cavity depth), there is space for:

  • Power and HDMI cable routing
  • Minor wall irregularities
  • Mounting bracket clearance

Physical Standoff Frame

For consistent mounting height, you can install a standoff frame. With v5.3.0's front exhaust design, this is optional - there is no critical top gap to enforce:

Standoff Frame (Optional for v5.3.0):

  • Material: Aluminum L-angle (25×25×3mm) or steel
  • Position: Fixed to cavity sides at 20mm below top of cavity
  • Function: Provides consistent mounting height (convenience, not thermal)
  • Note: With front exhaust, standoff is optional - no thermal penalty if omitted

4. Installation Do's and Don'ts

✓ DO

  • Verify cavity dimensions BEFORE ordering
  • Allow 20mm fitting tolerance at top
  • Ensure bottom of enclosure has clear airflow
  • Keep front bezel vents unobstructed
  • Route cables to sides (not blocking front vents)
  • Take photos of cavity before and after install

✗ DON'T

  • Block front bezel vents with trim or covers
  • Cover bottom intake slots
  • Route cables across front bezel vents
  • Install without measuring cavity depth
  • Tape over vents "to keep dust out"
  • Reduce cavity depth below 215mm
v5.3.0 Front Exhaust Advantage Unlike rear-exhaust designs, the v5.3.0 can be installed nearly flush to the top of the cavity without thermal penalty. Hot air exits through the front bezel, not into the cavity. The 20mm gap is only for fitting tolerance - not a thermal requirement.

5. Pre-Installation Checklist

  • Cavity width matches specification (±5mm)
  • Cavity height allows 20mm fitting tolerance at top
  • Cavity depth is minimum 215mm
  • Front of cavity clear (no trim blocking bezel vents)
  • Power outlet positioned to side (not behind enclosure)
  • HDMI/cable conduit routed to side
  • No pipes, wires, or obstructions in cavity
  • Cavity surface is flat (no bulges reducing depth)
  • Cavity is waterproofed if in wet area
  • Photos taken of empty cavity for records

6. Wall-Mount Alternative

Wall-mount installations are simpler If recess cavity cannot meet specifications, consider surface wall-mount instead:
  • Minimum 30mm clearance from wall (provided by standard bracket)
  • No top gap requirement (open air above)
  • Easier cable access
  • Better thermal performance

7. Warranty Implications

Warranty Void Conditions:
  • Front bezel vents (intake or exhaust) blocked or covered
  • Cavity depth less than 215mm (insufficient cable space)
  • Evidence of thermal damage due to restricted airflow
  • Modifications to ventilation system or gaskets
  • Installation without meeting minimum clearances

Required for warranty claims: Photos of installation showing front bezel vents are unobstructed, or installer certification that specifications were met.

Critical installation specifications for recessed wall mounting

CRITICAL: Incorrect cavity dimensions will cause thermal failure This enclosure requires specific clearances for proper airflow. Installers MUST follow these specifications exactly. Apex Enclosures warranty is void if clearance requirements are not met.

2. Why These Dimensions Matter

Top Gap (60mm minimum)

The enclosure exhausts hot air through the top bezel. This air must escape the cavity without recirculating back to the intake at the bottom.

  • Less than 60mm: Exhaust air recirculates → TV overheats → thermal shutdown or damage
  • Blocked top: Enclosure cannot cool → immediate thermal failure
  • 60mm+ gap: Hot air exhausts forward and upward, away from intake

Rear Depth (55mm minimum)

The enclosure is 160mm deep. With 55mm behind it (total 215mm cavity depth), there is space for:

  • Power and HDMI cable routing
  • Minor wall irregularities
  • Mounting bracket clearance

3. Standoff Frame System (Recommended)

Physical Standoff Frame

To prevent installation errors, we recommend installing a standoff frame that physically enforces the correct clearances:

Standoff frame ensures enclosure sits at correct height, enforcing 60mm top gap

Standoff Frame Specification:

  • Material: Aluminum L-angle (25×25×3mm) or steel
  • Position: Fixed to cavity sides at 60mm below top of cavity
  • Function: Enclosure rests on frame, cannot be installed higher
  • Benefit: Impossible to block exhaust gap - installation error-proof

4. Installation Do's and Don'ts

✓ DO

  • Verify cavity dimensions BEFORE ordering
  • Install standoff frame first
  • Leave 60mm gap at top
  • Ensure bottom intake is not obstructed
  • Route cables to sides, not blocking airflow
  • Take photos of cavity before and after install

✗ DON'T

  • Push enclosure flush to top of cavity
  • Block exhaust with finishing trim
  • Route cables across intake/exhaust
  • Install without measuring
  • Tape over vents "to keep dust out"
  • Reduce cavity depth to "save space"
Common Mistake: "Flush Top" Installation Contractors often push the enclosure to the top of the cavity for a "cleaner look." This blocks exhaust airflow and WILL cause thermal failure. The 60mm gap is not optional.

5. Pre-Installation Checklist

  • Cavity width matches specification (±5mm)
  • Cavity height matches specification (±5mm)
  • Cavity depth is minimum 215mm
  • Standoff frame installed at correct height
  • Power outlet positioned to side (not behind enclosure)
  • HDMI/cable conduit routed to side
  • No pipes, wires, or obstructions in cavity
  • Cavity surface is flat (no bulges reducing depth)
  • Cavity is waterproofed if in wet area
  • Photos taken of empty cavity for records

6. Wall-Mount Alternative

Wall-mount installations are simpler If recess cavity cannot meet specifications, consider surface wall-mount instead:
  • Minimum 30mm clearance from wall (provided by standard bracket)
  • No top gap requirement (open air above)
  • Easier cable access
  • Better thermal performance

7. Warranty Implications

Warranty Void Conditions:
  • Enclosure installed with less than 60mm top clearance
  • Intake or exhaust vents blocked or covered
  • Cavity depth less than 215mm
  • Evidence of thermal damage due to restricted airflow
  • Modifications to ventilation system

Required for warranty claims: Photos of installation showing clearances, or installer certification that specifications were met.

Drawing Style Comparison

Side cross-section of ATE-75 v5.2.2 IP55 shown in three different styles

1. Engineering Line Drawing Technical

Black/white orthographic projection with dimension lines. Standard for manufacturing.

+ Industry standard for manufacturing

+ Clear dimensions, unambiguous

+ Prints well in B&W

- Less visually engaging

- Harder to understand airflow at a glance

2. Clean 2D Schematic Current Style

Simplified colors, clear labels. Good for presentations and web.

+ Easy to understand at a glance

+ Airflow path is clear

+ Good for web/presentations

- Not precise enough for manufacturing

- Colors may not print well

3. Isometric View 3D-ish

3D perspective line drawing. Shows spatial relationships.

+ Shows 3D spatial relationships

+ Easier to visualize assembly

+ Good for cutaway views

- Dimensions less clear

- More complex to create accurately

- Can be confusing without shading

Recommendation

For manufacturing RFQ: Style 1 (Engineering)
For website/sales: Style 2 (Clean Schematic) or Style 3 (Isometric)
Best balance: Style 2 with key dimensions added

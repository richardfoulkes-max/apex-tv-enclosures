Critical Design Challenge: Top Exhaust Airflow

Key Finding: The ROTEAM Europa uses TOP-mounted exhaust fans, not side airflow. This requires a fundamentally different enclosure design with an acoustic chimney/plenum.

Airflow Pattern

Heat pumps draw air through side/rear coils and exhaust vertically through top-mounted fans. The enclosure must:

  • Allow unrestricted air intake through acoustic louvres on multiple sides
  • Provide a clear vertical exhaust path above the unit
  • Include an acoustic chimney to attenuate exhaust noise
  • Maintain sufficient airflow to prevent overheating

Airflow Requirements Calculation

Step 1: Estimate Required Airflow

Heat pumps typically require 200-300 m³/h per kW of capacity for proper heat exchange.

Airflow = Capacity x Airflow Factor

Conservative estimate (250 m³/h per kW):
Airflow = 44 kW x 250 m³/h/kW
Airflow = 11,000 m³/h

Step 2: Calculate Required Louvre Area

Acoustic louvres typically allow 2-3 m/s face velocity while maintaining acoustic performance.

Area = Airflow / (Velocity x 3600)

At 1.5 m/s (conservative for acoustics):
Area = 11,000 / (1.5 x 3600)
Area = 11,000 / 5,400
Area = 2.04 m² minimum

At 2.0 m/s (moderate):
Area = 11,000 / (2.0 x 3600)
Area = 1.53 m² minimum
Design Target: Provide minimum 2.0 m² of acoustic louvre area for intake, plus adequate exhaust area in the chimney.

Step 3: Pressure Drop Considerations

Total system pressure drop must not exceed fan capability:

Component Estimated Pressure Drop
Intake acoustic louvres (Z-blade) 15-25 Pa
Acoustic chimney plenum 10-20 Pa
Exhaust acoustic louvres 15-25 Pa
Total Additional 40-70 Pa
Acceptable: Heat pump fans typically have 100-200 Pa static pressure capability. Additional 40-70 Pa from enclosure should be manageable with properly sized louvres.

Louvre Area Calculation

Intake Louvres (3 sides):
Front: 1400 x 700 mm = 0.98 m²
Left: 900 x 700 mm = 0.63 m²
Right: 900 x 700 mm = 0.63 m²
Total Intake: 2.24 m²

Exhaust Louvres (chimney top):
Top: 1400 x 900 mm = 1.26 m²
Exhaust: 1.26 m²

With 50% free area louvres:
Effective intake: 2.24 x 0.5 = 1.12 m²
Effective exhaust: 1.26 x 0.5 = 0.63 m²
Design Adjustment Needed: With 50% free area louvres, effective intake area (1.12 m²) is below the 1.5 m² target. Options:
  • Increase enclosure height to add more louvre area
  • Use higher free-area louvres (60-70%)
  • Add louvres to rear panel (if accessible)
  • Accept higher face velocity (2.5-3 m/s) with slight acoustic penalty

Acoustic Chimney Design

The acoustic chimney is the critical component for attenuating exhaust noise while allowing unrestricted vertical airflow.

Design Principles

  • Length: Minimum 600mm for effective low-frequency attenuation
  • Lining: 50mm mineral wool on all four internal faces
  • Cross-section: Maintain or exceed fan outlet area
  • Top: Weather-protected acoustic louvres or baffled outlet

Chimney Construction

Temperature Rise Calculation

Delta T = Q / (m x Cp)

Where:
Q = 5 kW (heat input)
m = 11,000 m³/h x 1.2 kg/m³ = 13,200 kg/h = 3.67 kg/s
Cp = 1.005 kJ/kg·K (air)

Delta T = 5 / (3.67 x 1.005)
Delta T = 1.4°C
Excellent Result: With proper airflow maintained, air temperature rise through enclosure is only ~1-2°C. The heat pump will operate within normal parameters.

Worst Case: Reduced Airflow

If airflow is reduced by 50% due to clogged louvres or design issues:

At 50% airflow (5,500 m³/h):
m = 1.83 kg/s
Delta T = 5 / (1.83 x 1.005)
Delta T = 2.7°C

Still acceptable, but louvre maintenance becomes important.

Key Design Features

  • Acoustic chimney: 600mm plenum with mineral wool lining for exhaust noise attenuation
  • Three-sided louvres: Z-blade acoustic louvres on front, left, and right for intake
  • Double-skin panels: 2mm outer + 50mm mineral wool + 1mm perforated inner

  • Weather protection: Angled top on chimney to shed rain
  • Powder coat finish: RAL 7016 or custom colour, marine grade
Validation Complete: The revised APE-HC1 design provides adequate airflow for the ROTEAM Europa 44kW heat pump while achieving 15-20 dB(A) noise reduction. The acoustic chimney is essential for this top-exhaust unit configuration.

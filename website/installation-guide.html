Wall Mounting

1

Mark the Bracket Position

Hold the wall bracket at your chosen height. Use a spirit level to ensure it's perfectly horizontal. Mark all fixing holes with a pencil.

i
Recommended Height Center of screen at seated eye level (typically 1000-1200mm from floor to bracket center).
2

Drill Pilot Holes

Using a 10mm masonry bit, drill holes to 90mm depth at each marked position. Clear dust from holes with a brush or compressed air.

3

Install Wall Anchors

Insert the M10 concrete anchors into the drilled holes. Tap gently with a hammer until flush with the wall surface.

4

Secure the Bracket

Position the bracket over the anchors. Insert bolts through bracket into anchors. Tighten with 17mm socket — firm but do not overtighten. Check level one final time.

!
Important Tighten bolts in a star pattern (opposite corners) to ensure even pressure. Target torque: 25-30 Nm.
5

Hang the Enclosure

Two people required. Lift the enclosure and hook it onto the wall bracket. The enclosure should click into place. Verify it's secure before releasing.

4

TV Installation

1

Open the Service Door

Release the four latches on the bottom bezel by turning them 90° counter-clockwise. The service door will swing down on its hinges.

2

Attach VESA Rails to TV

Using the M6 or M8 bolts (depending on your TV), attach the VESA adapter rails to the back of your TV. Ensure rails are level and secure.

TV SizeCommon VESA PatternBolt Size
65"400x400mmM8 x 25mm
75"400x400mm or 600x400mmM8 x 25mm
77" OLED300x300mmM6 x 15mm
3

Slide TV into Enclosure

Two people required. Carefully lift the TV and slide the VESA rails into the vertical mounting channels inside the enclosure. Lower until the rails lock into position.

4

Connect Cables

Route cables through the cable entry point at the rear. Connect:

  • Power cable to TV
  • HDMI cable(s) to TV
  • IR extender cable (place receiver on front glass)
  • Ethernet (optional)
5

Close and Seal

Swing the service door up and secure all four latches by turning them 90° clockwise. Ensure the EPDM gasket is properly seated all around.

5

Electrical Connection

!
Warning Electrical work should be performed by a licensed electrician. Ensure the circuit is rated for the total load (TV + enclosure fans = typically 400-500W).

Connect Enclosure Power

Plug the enclosure's power cable into a weatherproof outdoor socket (IP44 or higher). The enclosure uses a standard 3-pin plug.

i
Power Location Ideally, power outlet should be behind or below the enclosure for a clean cable run. Maximum recommended cable run: 3 meters.
6

Testing & Handover

Installation Checklist

Enclosure securely mounted and level
TV powers on and displays correctly
All HDMI sources working
IR extender responds to remote
Fans spin up when powered (brief test at startup)
Service door latches secure
No cable pinching at entry points
Glass clean and free of fingerprints
*
Congratulations! Your Apex enclosure is installed. For the first week, we recommend checking the enclosure daily to ensure everything is operating correctly. The fans will automatically adjust speed based on temperature.

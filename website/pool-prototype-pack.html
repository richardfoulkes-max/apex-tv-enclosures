1.2 Design Philosophy

Why Modular?

Unlike TV enclosures (standardized sizes), pool equipment varies dramatically. A modular system with base units and extensions solves the "every setup is different" problem without requiring custom fabrication for each customer.

Why Acoustic?

Pool pumps generate 65-80 dB at 1m - louder than a vacuum cleaner. In villa communities, noise complaints are common. Acoustic lining differentiates from DIY solutions and commands premium pricing.

1.3 Target Market

  • Primary: UAE/GCC luxury villa owners (Emirates Hills, Palm Jumeirah, Al Barari)
  • Secondary: Hotel/resort pool areas, community pool managers
  • Channel: Pool contractors (Medley Pool, Astralux), landscape architects

5.2 Rain-Proof Acoustic Baffle Design

All ventilation openings use downward-angled louvers that block rain while maintaining airflow and acoustic performance.

Intake Louvers (Bottom/Sides)

  • Louvered design (30° downward angle)
  • Double-layer with 25mm stagger
  • Drip edges on each louver blade
  • Foam-lined internal surfaces
  • Effective open area: 40%
  • Sound reduction: 15-20 dB
  • Rain ingress: Zero

Exhaust Louvers (Top)

  • Louvered design (45° downward angle)
  • 75mm deep louver blades
  • Foam strips on louver backs
  • Overlapping design blocks vertical rain
  • Sound reduction: 10-15 dB
  • Rain ingress: Zero

5.3 Multi-Layer Acoustic Strategy

LayerComponentAttenuationCumulative
1Acoustic louvre (intake)12-15 dB12-15 dB
2Internal absorption (walls)3-5 dB15-20 dB
3Acoustic louvre (exhaust)12-15 dB27-35 dB
4Mass barrier (flanking paths)15-20 dB-
Practical Result15-20 dB overall reduction
Why not 35 dB? Real-world performance is limited by flanking paths (sound leaking around barriers), imperfect seals, and low-frequency content. Our 15-20 dB target is achievable and meaningful - it represents 70-80% perceived noise reduction.

9.4 Acoustic Testing Protocol

  1. Measure heat pump noise without enclosure (1m distance, A-weighted)
  2. Install enclosure, repeat measurement at same position
  3. Calculate insertion loss (difference)
  4. Check at multiple angles (front, side, rear)
  5. Verify equipment operating temperature is within spec

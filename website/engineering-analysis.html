2. Thermal Analysis - TV-Like Profile

2.1 Heat Load (Unchanged)

// === HEAT SOURCES - ATE-75 (TV-Like Profile) === // Heat Source 1: TV Power Dissipation 75" LED TV typical power: 180W Power converted to heat: ~95% = 171W // Heat Source 2: Solar Radiation Dubai peak irradiance: 1000 W/m² Glass area (75"): 1.55 m² IR rejection (3M PR90 film): 90% blocked Solar heat after IR film: 1000 × 1.55 × 0.45 × 0.10 = 70W Visible light absorption: 1000 × 1.55 × 0.50 × 0.10 = 78W Total solar load: 148W // Heat Source 3: Electronics Controller + PSU losses: 6W // === TOTAL HEAT LOAD === TV: 171W Solar: 148W Electronics: 6W Safety margin (10%): 33W ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ DESIGN HEAT LOAD: 358W (~350W rounded)

2.2 Cooling Capacity - New Configuration

// === FAN SPECIFICATIONS (4× 80mm PWM - e.g., Noctua NF-A8) === Fan size: 80mm × 80mm × 25mm Free air CFM per fan: 32 CFM (typical quality 80mm) Max static pressure: 2.37 mm H₂O (23 Pa) Quantity: 4 fans // === SYSTEM PRESSURE DROP (TV-Like Profile) === Bottom intake slot (filtered): 12 Pa Internal flow resistance: 5 Pa Rear exhaust cowls: 8 Pa ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ Total system impedance: ~25 Pa // === OPERATING POINT === At 25 Pa (near max pressure for 80mm fans): Per fan CFM: ~25 CFM Four fans: 100 CFM = 0.047 m³/s // === NATURAL CONVECTION ASSIST === Bottom-to-rear airflow path: - Hot air naturally rises - Creates pressure differential (~3-5 Pa assist) - Effective boost to fan performance: +20-30% Effective total airflow: ~120-130 CFM = 0.057 m³/s

2.3 Temperature Rise Calculation

// === FORCED CONVECTION HEAT TRANSFER === Formula: Q = ṁ × Cp × ΔT Rearranged: ΔT = Q / (ṁ × Cp) Where: Q = 350W (heat load) ṁ = 0.057 m³/s × 1.09 kg/m³ = 0.062 kg/s (with convection assist) Cp = 1005 J/(kg·K) ΔT = 350 / (0.062 × 1005) ΔT = 5.6°C (air temperature rise through enclosure) // === WITHOUT CONVECTION ASSIST (fans only) === ṁ = 0.047 × 1.09 = 0.051 kg/s ΔT = 350 / (0.051 × 1005) ΔT = 6.8°C (higher without natural convection) // === INTERNAL AIR TEMPERATURE === Ambient (Dubai worst case): 55°C Air temperature rise: +5.6°C ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ Internal air (fans + convection): 60.6°C

2.4 Heat Dissipation Through Enclosure

// === CONDUCTIVE/RADIATIVE LOSSES (Aluminum Enclosure) === Enclosure surface area: - Front: 1.76 × 1.04 = 1.83 m² - Back: 1.83 m² (with fan cutouts ~1.6 m² effective) - Sides: 2 × (1.04 × 0.115) = 0.24 m² - Top/Bottom: 2 × (1.76 × 0.115) = 0.40 m² Total surface: ~4.0 m² // Convective heat transfer from enclosure surface: h (natural convection, vertical): 5-10 W/(m²·K) h (with wind): 15-25 W/(m²·K) Conservative estimate (light breeze, h = 12): Q_surface = h × A × ΔT If internal air = 60.6°C, ambient = 55°C, ΔT = 5.6°C Q_surface = 12 × 4.0 × 5.6 = 269W dissipated through enclosure // With white/sand powder coat (high emissivity ~0.9): Radiative losses add: ~50-80W // === THERMAL EQUILIBRIUM === Heat in: 350W Heat out (forced convection): 350W (air carries it out) Heat out (enclosure surface): 269W + 65W = 334W (parallel path) Note: Both paths work in parallel - not additive! The enclosure reaches equilibrium where total heat out = heat in // === REALISTIC INTERNAL TEMPERATURE === With proper airflow (wall mount): - Forced convection dominates - Internal air: 58-62°C - TV back panel: ~+5°C above air = 63-67°C ⚠ CONCERN: TV back panel at 63-67°C exceeds typical 50°C limit!

2.5 TV Operating Temperature Analysis

// === TV THERMAL LIMITS === Consumer TV (Samsung/LG) rated ambient: 0-35°C (operating) Commercial display rated ambient: 0-50°C (operating) TV back panel max (typical): 50°C // === PROBLEM ANALYSIS === With 55°C ambient + 350W heat load: Internal air temperature: 58-62°C TV back panel temperature: 63-67°C (EXCEEDS 50°C LIMIT) // === MITIGATION STRATEGIES === Strategy 1: Direct airflow over TV back - Position fans to blow directly across TV back panel - Reduces TV surface temp by 8-12°C - TV back panel: 55-58°C (still marginal) Strategy 2: White/light enclosure color - Reflects ~60% solar vs 5% for black - Reduces enclosure surface temp by 8-12°C - Reduces internal air by ~5-7°C - TV back panel: 50-55°C (acceptable with direct airflow) Strategy 3: Enhanced IR rejection (90% minimum) - Already specified - saves ~150W heat load - Without this, design would FAIL Strategy 4: Thermal interface between TV and airflow - Ensure 15mm gap behind TV for airflow - Critical for convective cooling of TV back // === REVISED PREDICTION WITH ALL MITIGATIONS === White enclosure + direct airflow + 90% IR: Internal air: 52-56°C TV back panel: 48-52°C ✓ MARGINAL PASS (with all mitigations)
THERMAL ANALYSIS: MARGINAL PASS (Wall Mount)

Conclusion: The TV-like profile with 4×80mm fans provides ~100 CFM airflow (vs ~320 CFM with previous design). This is adequate for wall-mounted installations with ALL mitigations applied:

  • MANDATORY: White/sand powder coat (NOT black)
  • MANDATORY: 90% IR rejection film
  • MANDATORY: Direct airflow path over TV back (15mm gap minimum)
  • MANDATORY: Fans positioned to exhaust hot air from TV back area

Margin: ~2-5°C margin at 55°C ambient. Design is thermal-limited.

3. CRITICAL: Recessed Mounting Analysis

Installation Scenario: Customer wants to mount the enclosure in a wall recess (niche) for a flush, built-in appearance. This SIGNIFICANTLY impacts thermal performance.

3.1 Recessed Mount - Airflow Problems

Recessed mounting blocks both intake AND exhaust!

3.2 Recessed Mount Thermal Failure Analysis

// === RECESSED MOUNTING SCENARIO === Recess depth: 150mm (enclosure 115mm + 35mm clearance) Side clearance: 10mm each side Bottom clearance: 0mm (sitting on shelf) Top clearance: 20mm // === AIRFLOW IMPACT === Bottom intake slot: 100% BLOCKED (sitting on surface) Rear exhaust: 90% BLOCKED (35mm gap, no airflow) Natural convection: SEVERELY LIMITED (enclosed space) Effective airflow: ~5-10 CFM (recirculating) // === TEMPERATURE RISE (RECESSED) === Heat load: 350W (unchanged) Effective airflow: ~7 CFM = 0.003 m³/s ṁ = 0.003 × 1.09 = 0.003 kg/s ΔT = 350 / (0.003 × 1005) ΔT = 116°C (THERMAL RUNAWAY!) // === WHAT ACTUALLY HAPPENS === 1. Fans run at 100% 2. Air recirculates inside recess 3. Recess air heats to 70-80°C 4. TV back panel reaches 80-90°C 5. TV thermal shutdown (or damage) 6. Enclosure internal temp exceeds 85°C 7. PRODUCT FAILURE // === MINIMUM CLEARANCES FOR RECESSED MOUNT === To achieve adequate airflow in recess: Bottom clearance (intake): 100mm MINIMUM (allow air entry) Rear clearance (exhaust): 150mm MINIMUM (allow hot air exit) Side clearance: 50mm each side (allow circulation) Top clearance: 100mm (allow convection) These clearances make "flush recess" aesthetically pointless!
RECESSED MOUNTING: FAILS WITHOUT MODIFICATIONS

Problem: Standard recessed mounting blocks the bottom intake and rear exhaust, causing thermal runaway and product failure.

Root Cause: TV-like profile design relies on bottom-to-rear airflow that is completely blocked in a typical recess.

3.3 Recessed Mounting Solutions

Solution A: Ventilated Recess Design

Modify the wall recess to allow airflow:

Bottom:Install grille/slot in shelf below enclosure (200×50mm opening)
Rear:Leave 150mm cavity behind enclosure connected to ceiling void
Top:Install exhaust grille in ceiling of recess

Effectiveness: Restores ~60% of normal airflow. Acceptable for moderate climates.

Suitability for Dubai 55°C: MARGINAL - may still overheat on hottest days.

Solution B: Active Cooling Duct (Recommended for Dubai)

Install ducted HVAC supply to the recess:

Supply:100mm flex duct from AC system to bottom of recess
Flow:50-100 CFM of conditioned air (24°C)
Exhaust:Passive grille to ceiling void or return duct
// === DUCTED COOLING CALCULATION === Conditioned air supply: 24°C @ 75 CFM Heat load: 350W ΔT = 350 / (0.035 × 1005) = 10°C Exhaust air temperature: 24 + 10 = 34°C TV back panel: ~38-42°C (EXCELLENT!) ✓ FULLY COMPLIANT - Best solution for recessed install

Cost: ~AED 500-1000 (ductwork + grilles)

Suitability for Dubai 55°C: EXCELLENT - recommended approach.

Solution C: Supplementary External Fan Unit

Install external booster fan system:

Configuration:2× 120mm fans mounted in bottom shelf of recess
Airflow:~100 CFM forced through bottom grille
Exhaust:Grille in top of recess to ceiling void

Effectiveness: Restores full airflow capacity.

Drawbacks: Additional noise, power consumption, maintenance.

Suitability for Dubai 55°C: ACCEPTABLE - functional but not elegant.

Solution D: Alternative Product - Surface Mount Bracket

Use wall-mount bracket that holds enclosure away from wall:

Stand-off:150mm from wall surface
Bottom clearance:100mm above any shelf/obstruction
Appearance:Visible bracket, not recessed

Effectiveness: Full airflow, original design intent.

Drawback: Not flush-mounted aesthetic.

Suitability for Dubai 55°C: OPTIMAL - design operates as intended.

5. Airflow Physics - Bottom-to-Rear Configuration

5.1 Natural Convection Enhancement

// === WHY BOTTOM-TO-REAR IS BETTER THAN SIDE-TO-TOP === Principle: Hot air rises naturally (buoyancy) // Previous design (side intake → top exhaust): - Air enters horizontally at sides - Must be forced upward by fans - Fights against natural stratification inside - Natural convection provides minimal assist // New design (bottom intake → rear exhaust): - Cool air enters at bottom (densest, naturally sinks there) - Air heated by TV rises naturally - Exits at rear (upper section preferred) - Natural convection ASSISTS fan operation // === BUOYANCY CALCULATION === Internal temp: 60°C Ambient temp: 55°C ΔT: 5°C Height of enclosure: 1.04m Buoyancy pressure (stack effect): ΔP = ρ × g × h × ΔT/T ΔP = 1.09 × 9.81 × 1.04 × 5/328 ΔP ≈ 0.17 Pa Not huge, but in same direction as fans = helpful! At higher internal temps (e.g., 70°C, ΔT=15°C): ΔP ≈ 0.5 Pa - more significant assist Net effect: 20-30% improvement in effective airflow vs fighting convection in side-to-top design

5.2 Internal Airflow Path

Bottom-to-rear airflow: Cool air enters bottom, rises past TV, exits rear. Natural convection assists fan operation.

5.3 Filter Service Interval

// === FILTER LOADING - Reduced Airflow === Previous airflow: 320 CFM New airflow: 100 CFM Ratio: 0.31× (69% less air = 69% less dust) // Previous filter change interval: ~30 days in dusty conditions // New interval: 30 / 0.31 = ~97 days // However, smaller intake area concentrates dust: Bottom slot area: ~0.05 m² vs 0.09 m² before Concentration factor: 1.8× Adjusted interval: 97 / 1.8 = ~54 days Recommendation: Check filter every 6-8 weeks in Dubai
AIRFLOW ANALYSIS: PASS (Wall Mount)

Bottom-to-rear airflow with natural convection assist provides adequate cooling for wall-mounted installations. The 100 CFM + convection design works because:

  • Reduced heat load (90% IR film blocks 627W of solar)
  • Natural convection assists rather than fights the fans
  • Direct airflow path over TV back panel
  • White enclosure color reduces surface temperature

6.2 Installation Guidelines

WALL MOUNT: APPROVED

Standard wall mount with 150mm stand-off from wall. Ensure:

  • 100mm clearance below enclosure for intake
  • 150mm clearance behind for exhaust
  • No obstructions within 300mm of intake/exhaust
RECESSED MOUNT: CONDITIONAL APPROVAL

For Dubai/Gulf climate (55°C ambient):

  • Standard recess: NOT APPROVED
  • Ducted recess (AC supply): APPROVED - recommended solution
  • Ventilated recess: NOT RECOMMENDED - marginal performance

Customer guidance: If customer requires recessed mounting in Dubai, quote additional AED 500-1000 for ducted installation. Without ducting, warranty is void for thermal damage.

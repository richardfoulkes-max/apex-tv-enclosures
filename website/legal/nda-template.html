WHEREAS: Apex is engaged in the design and commercialization of outdoor TV enclosures and related products; and Manufacturer is engaged in the fabrication of metal enclosures and related manufacturing services; and the Parties wish to explore a potential business relationship involving the manufacture of Apex's proprietary products; and in the course of such discussions, the Parties may disclose confidential information to each other.

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants contained herein, the Parties agree as follows:

1. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

1.1 "Confidential Information" means any and all information disclosed by either Party to the other, whether orally, in writing, or by any other means, that relates to:

  1. Product designs, specifications, drawings, CAD files, and technical documentation
  2. Manufacturing processes, techniques, and methods
  3. Bill of materials, component specifications, and supplier information
  4. Business plans, pricing strategies, cost structures, and financial information
  5. Customer lists, market research, and sales strategies
  6. Trade secrets, know-how, and proprietary technology
  7. Any information marked as "Confidential" or that should reasonably be understood to be confidential given its nature

1.2 Confidential Information does not include information that:

  1. Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the receiving Party
  2. Was rightfully in the receiving Party's possession before disclosure
  3. Is rightfully obtained from a third party without breach of any confidentiality obligation
  4. Is independently developed by the receiving Party without use of Confidential Information
  5. Is required to be disclosed by law, provided prompt notice is given to the disclosing Party

2. OBLIGATIONS OF CONFIDENTIALITY

2.1 Each Party agrees to:

  1. Maintain the confidentiality of the other Party's Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care used to protect its own confidential information, but not less than reasonable care
  2. Use the Confidential Information solely for the purpose of evaluating and pursuing the potential business relationship between the Parties (the "Purpose")
  3. Not disclose the Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent
  4. Limit access to Confidential Information to employees and contractors who have a need to know and are bound by confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than this Agreement
  5. Not copy, reproduce, or reverse engineer any Confidential Information except as necessary for the Purpose

3. NON-COMPETE AND NON-CIRCUMVENTION

3.1 Non-Compete: Manufacturer agrees that during the term of this Agreement and for a period of three (3) years thereafter, Manufacturer shall not:

  1. Design, manufacture, market, or sell outdoor TV enclosures or substantially similar products that compete with Apex's products
  2. Use Apex's Confidential Information to develop competing products for any third party
  3. Assist any third party in developing products that compete with Apex's products using knowledge gained through this relationship

3.2 Non-Circumvention: Manufacturer agrees not to:

  1. Contact or solicit Apex's customers, suppliers, or business contacts identified through this relationship
  2. Interfere with Apex's business relationships
  3. Attempt to bypass Apex to deal directly with any third party introduced through this relationship

4. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

4.1 All Confidential Information remains the exclusive property of the disclosing Party.

4.2 No license or right, express or implied, is granted under any patent, trademark, copyright, or other intellectual property right by virtue of this Agreement or any disclosure of Confidential Information.

4.3 Any improvements, modifications, or derivative works developed by Manufacturer using Apex's Confidential Information shall be the sole property of Apex.

4.4 Manufacturer shall not file any patent applications or seek any intellectual property protection for designs, inventions, or improvements derived from Apex's Confidential Information.

5. RETURN OF MATERIALS

5.1 Upon termination of this Agreement or upon request by the disclosing Party, the receiving Party shall promptly:

  1. Return all documents, materials, and tangible items containing Confidential Information
  2. Permanently delete all electronic copies of Confidential Information
  3. Certify in writing that all Confidential Information has been returned or destroyed

5.2 Notwithstanding the foregoing, the receiving Party may retain one archival copy solely for legal compliance purposes, subject to the ongoing confidentiality obligations of this Agreement.

6. TERM AND TERMINATION

6.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of five (5) years, unless terminated earlier by either Party with 30 days written notice.

6.2 The confidentiality obligations under this Agreement shall survive termination for a period of five (5) years from the date of termination.

6.3 The non-compete obligations under Section 3 shall survive termination for a period of three (3) years from the date of termination.

7. REMEDIES

7.1 The Parties acknowledge that a breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be an inadequate remedy.

7.2 In addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity, the non-breaching Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief to prevent any breach or threatened breach of this Agreement.

7.3 The prevailing Party in any action to enforce this Agreement shall be entitled to recover reasonable attorneys' fees and costs.

8. GENERAL PROVISIONS

8.1 Governing Law: This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the United Arab Emirates.

8.2 Dispute Resolution: Any disputes arising out of this Agreement shall be resolved through binding arbitration in Dubai, UAE, in accordance with the rules of the Dubai International Arbitration Centre.

8.3 Entire Agreement: This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior negotiations, agreements, and understandings.

8.4 Amendment: This Agreement may only be amended by a written instrument signed by both Parties.

8.5 Assignment: Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other Party.

8.6 Severability: If any provision of this Agreement is found to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

8.7 Waiver: No failure or delay in exercising any right under this Agreement shall operate as a waiver thereof.

8.8 Notices: All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and sent to the addresses set forth above.

9. ACKNOWLEDGMENT

The Parties acknowledge that they have read and understood this Agreement, have had the opportunity to seek independent legal advice, and agree to be bound by its terms.

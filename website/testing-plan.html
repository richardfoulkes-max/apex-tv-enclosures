3. Test Phases

1 Pre-Installation Inspection (Day 0)

Before installing the TV, verify enclosure build quality.

  • Dimensional check: Verify internal dimensions match spec (within ±3mm)
  • Gasket inspection: EPDM gasket continuous, no gaps, properly seated
  • Door operation: Hinges smooth, gas struts hold door open, latches engage fully
  • Cable gland check: IP66 glands installed, threads sealed
  • Fan operation: Power on, verify all fans spin, no rattling or grinding
  • Controller test: Verify PWM responds to temperature input (use heat gun briefly)
  • Powder coat quality: No chips, scratches, or thin spots
  • Drainage holes: Verify drain holes are clear (bottom of enclosure)

2 Thermal Validation - Indoor (Days 1-2)

Establish baseline thermal performance in controlled environment before outdoor exposure.

Sensor Placement

Channel Location Purpose
T1 TV back panel center Critical - TV operating temp
T2 Internal air (mid-height) Enclosure air temperature
T3 Intake air (at filter) Ambient reference
T4 Exhaust air (at fan outlet) Delta-T calculation

Indoor Test Protocol

  • Install TV and sensors - Route sensor wires through cable glands
  • Power on TV - Display bright content (white screen or video loop)
  • Log temperatures every 5 minutes for 4 hours
  • Record steady-state temps (after ~2 hours when stable)
  • Calculate delta-T: T4 - T3 should be 2-5°C (validates airflow)
  • Verify fan speed increases as T2 rises above 30°C
Pass Criteria (Indoor @ 25°C ambient):
T1 (TV back) < 40°C | T2 (internal air) < 35°C | Delta-T (T4-T3) = 2-5°C

3 Thermal Validation - Outdoor (Days 3-14)

The critical test: real Gulf climate conditions with solar load.

Installation Requirements

  • Location: Outdoor, south-facing (maximum sun exposure)
  • Mounting: Secure to wall or sturdy frame at viewing height
  • Power: Outdoor-rated extension or dedicated circuit with GFCI
  • Data logging: Configure logger for 5-minute intervals, 24/7
  • TV operation: Run 8+ hours daily (peak sun hours mandatory)

Daily Monitoring Checklist

Time Action Record
08:00 Power on TV, start video loop Ambient temp, weather conditions
12:00-14:00 Peak sun observation Max temps, fan speed (audible check)
14:00 Photo of display (check for dimming/artifacts) Any visual anomalies
18:00 End of day check Download data, check for condensation
STOP TEST IMMEDIATELY IF:
- T1 (TV back) exceeds 50°C
- TV displays thermal warning or shuts down
- Visible smoke, burning smell, or component damage
- Any water ingress detected inside enclosure

Pass/Fail Criteria

Metric Target Acceptable Fail
T1 (TV back panel) < 40°C < 45°C > 50°C
T2 (internal air) < 35°C < 42°C > 48°C
TV operation No issues Minor dimming OK Shutdown / artifacts
Fan noise @ 100% < 45 dB < 55 dB > 60 dB

4 IP66 Water Ingress Test (Day 10)

Verify weatherproofing under simulated rain conditions.

Test Protocol (IEC 60529 Simplified)

  • Power off TV - Remove from enclosure if concerned about damage
  • Place absorbent paper inside enclosure floor and on all electronics
  • Seal door completely - All latches engaged
  • Spray test: Garden hose at 3m distance, medium pressure, 3 minutes per face
  • Test all faces: Front glass, top, both sides, door seams, cable entry
  • Wait 10 minutes after spray test
  • Open and inspect: Check all absorbent paper for moisture
Pass Criteria: No water penetration beyond drain channels. Paper remains dry. Electronics show no moisture.

Dust Ingress (Visual)

After 14 days outdoor exposure, inspect filter and interior:

  • Filter shows dust accumulation (expected - means it's working)
  • Interior surfaces clean (no dust bypassing filter)
  • Fan blades show minimal dust
  • Electronics compartment dust-free

5 Electrical Safety Verification (Day 1 & 14)

Initial Checks

  • Wiring inspection: All connections secure, no exposed copper
  • Grounding: Enclosure bonded to earth ground
  • Fuse rating: Correct fuse installed per spec (5A for fans/controller)
  • Power consumption: Measure with power meter, compare to calculated

End-of-Test Checks

  • No tripped breakers during 14-day test
  • No burned/melted wires or connectors
  • Surge protection: SPD indicator shows normal
  • Cable gland integrity: No heat damage to glands

6 Durability & Usability (Days 7-14)

Mechanical Durability

  • Door cycle test: Open/close door 50 times, check hinge wear
  • Latch engagement: All latches still engage properly after cycling
  • Gas strut function: Door stays open at all positions
  • Lock function: Cam locks and padlock hasps work correctly

Usability Assessment

  • TV visible in direct sunlight? (note: may need brightness boost)
  • Can one person service the unit (replace filter, access TV)?
  • IR remote works through glass?
  • Any annoying rattles or vibrations from fans?

5. If Tests Fail: Remediation Options

Failure Mode Likely Cause Remediation
T1 > 50°C (TV overheating) Insufficient airflow or solar load higher than calculated 1. Add 4th fan (upgrade to 4x 140mm)
2. Upgrade to 92% IR rejection film
3. Add shade/awning above enclosure
4. Limit operation to non-peak hours
Water ingress Gasket failure, unsealed penetrations 1. Replace gasket with thicker EPDM
2. Add silicone sealant at corners
3. Upgrade cable glands to IP68
4. Add drip edge above door
Fan failure / noise Poor quality fans, dust ingress 1. Upgrade to industrial IP68 fans
2. Improve filter (finer mesh)
3. Add fan guards
4. Use vibration dampening mounts
Condensation inside Humid air entering during cool cycle 1. Add silica gel desiccant packs
2. Add anti-condensation heater
3. Improve ventilation seal during off-hours
TV dimming/thermal throttle TV entering protection mode 1. All above cooling improvements
2. Consider outdoor-rated TV (higher temp tolerance)
3. Accept limitation or market as "shaded only"

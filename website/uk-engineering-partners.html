What to Send Them

Critical Requirement

We need a partner who can do BOTH electronics AND mechanical design integrated together. We have the components selected, but they need to:

  • Validate our component choices work together electrically
  • Design the control PCB / wiring harness
  • Position all electronics with proper clearances and thermal paths
  • Ensure the mechanical design accommodates the electrical requirements
  • Produce a complete, validated, manufacturing-ready package

What They Must Deliver

Electronics Design
  • Control PCB design (or validate off-shelf controller)
  • Wiring harness specification with connector types
  • Component validation (fans, sensors, PSU work together)
  • PWM control logic implementation
  • Cable routing and strain relief design
Thermal Validation
  • CFD or equivalent thermal analysis
  • Airflow validation (165-195 CFM target)
  • Hot spot identification and mitigation
  • Fan selection confirmation
Mechanical CAD
  • Complete 3D CAD model (STEP/IGES)
  • DXF files for sheet metal cutting
  • Assembly drawings with tolerances
  • Exploded view with part numbers
Manufacturing Package
  • BOM with supplier part numbers
  • Assembly instructions
  • QC checklist / test procedure
  • IP55 validation test plan

Recommended Next Steps

  1. Send initial email to Ignitec, Plextek, and 4D Products
  2. On call, confirm they do integrated electronics + mechanical - not just "we can put your components in a box"
  3. After scoping call - send full technical package (links above)
  4. Ask for: Proposal with scope, timeline, and budget
  5. Key question: "Will you validate our fan selection and control logic, or just position what we give you?"
  6. Red flag: If they say "just give us the electronics layout and we'll CAD it" - they're not the right partner

Questions to Ask on Scoping Call

  1. Do you have electronics engineers who can validate/design the control system, or just mechanical?
  2. Can you do thermal CFD analysis, or would that be outsourced?
  3. Have you designed products with active fan cooling and PWM control before?
  4. What IP-rated outdoor products have you delivered? Can we see examples?
  5. What CAD formats do you deliver? Can you produce DXF for sheet metal?
  6. What's your typical timeline for a project of this complexity?
  7. Do you have in-house prototyping, or would we need a separate manufacturer?

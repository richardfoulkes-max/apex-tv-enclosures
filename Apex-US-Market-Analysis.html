3. The 50% Tariff Problem

Section 232 Tariff: 50% duty on aluminum products from UAE. This is NOT going away anytime soon. Shipping from Eurotech to US is economically unviable.

Math: UAE vs US Manufacturing

Cost Component UAE → US US Domestic
Manufacturing $870 $1,100 - $1,400
Shipping $50 $50 - $100
50% Tariff $435 $0
Landed Cost ~$1,355 ~$1,150 - $1,500

Bottom line: Even with lower UAE manufacturing costs, the tariff wipes out any advantage. US manufacturing is required to compete.

6. Honest Assessment: Is US Worth It?

Arguments FOR pursuing US:

  • Massive market: 70% of global = $613M, growing 8-10%
  • Climate fit: Florida, Texas, Arizona = year-round outdoor, similar to Gulf
  • Premium positioning: Aluminum enclosure at mid-market price is underserved
  • Commercial opportunity: Hotels, resorts, sports bars (B2B with better margins)
  • Long-term play: If Apex wants to be a global brand, US is essential

Arguments AGAINST prioritizing US:

  • TV Shield dominance: US-made incumbent with 47,500 installations
  • High investment: $100K-200K to establish credible presence
  • No existing infrastructure: Manufacturing, distribution, support all from scratch
  • Brand awareness = zero: Will need significant marketing spend
  • Price pressure: Apollo at $1,100-1,400 sets floor
  • Distraction risk: Could pull focus from Gulf where Apex has advantages

Verdict: Not Now

The US is a long-term opportunity, not a short-term priority.

The market is real and large, but requires significant investment to compete against established US players. Apex should prove the model in Gulf first, then consider US entry once profitable.

1. Overview

Apex Enclosures develops and sells premium outdoor TV and pool equipment enclosures. This document outlines the partnership structure between the founding partners for operations across different territories.

Core Principle

Two separate business units with different ownership structures, reflecting each partner's contribution to building that territory.

4. Partner Roles & Responsibilities

Richard

Role: Product Development & IP

Responsibilities:

  • Product design and engineering specifications
  • Manufacturer relationships and quality control
  • Technical documentation and support
  • Brand development and marketing materials
  • Strategic direction across all territories

Tom

Role: Middle East Operations

Responsibilities:

  • UAE/GCC sales and customer relationships
  • Local operations and logistics
  • Capital contribution for Middle East operations
  • Manufacturer coordination (Eurotech)
  • Pool equipment product line development

Scott

Role: US / UK / EU Territory Development

Responsibilities:

  • Establish US and UK/EU sales operations
  • Identify and manage local manufacturing partners
  • Build distribution channels (direct, retail, commercial)
  • Customer support and returns handling in territory
  • Local marketing and brand building

5. Key Terms

Intellectual Property

  • All product IP, designs, and specifications remain owned by the Apex brand (Richard as originator)
  • Each territory has license to use IP for sales in their region
  • No partner can sell or license IP independently

Profit Distribution

  • Each territory operates as a separate P&L
  • Profits distributed according to equity split after expenses
  • Reinvestment decisions require majority approval within territory

Capital Contributions

  • Middle East: Richard and Tom contribute equally as needed
  • US/UK/EU: Investment to be discussed (Scott's sweat equity vs cash needs)
  • Additional capital calls require unanimous agreement

Exit & Transfer

  • Partners may not sell or transfer shares without right of first refusal to other partners
  • If a partner exits, remaining partners have option to buy at fair market value
  • Sale of entire business requires unanimous agreement

Decision Making

  • Day-to-day operations: Territory lead decides
  • Major decisions (new products, large investments): Majority of territory equity
  • Brand/IP decisions: Richard has final say

6. Next Steps

  1. Partners review and agree on this structure
  2. Decide on legal entity formation (holding company structure)
  3. Engage lawyer to draft formal partnership/shareholder agreement
  4. Set up banking and accounting for each territory
  5. Begin operations

Note

This is a summary of agreed principles, not a legal document. A formal partnership agreement should be drafted by a lawyer before any significant business activity or investment.
